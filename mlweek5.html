Unit 5: Clustering

Unit 5 focused on understanding the fundamentals of clustering and its applications in exploratory data analysis. I learned that clustering involves grouping objects into clusters so that items within the same cluster are more similar to each other than to those in other clusters. The unit explored the main techniques used in clustering, including K-means and agglomerative clustering, as well as different methods for measuring distances between data points. Additionally, I studied how to evaluate the quality of clusters and the common challenges and limitations associated with clustering techniques.

Key Learnings:

  • Understanding the basic concept and purpose of clustering in data analysis.
  • Learning different techniques for measuring distances between data points.
  • Exploring K-means and agglomerative clustering methods.
  • Evaluating clusters using appropriate metrics to determine their effectiveness.
  • Identifying skills needed to interpret and assess clustering results.
  • Understanding common pitfalls and limitations of clustering techniques.
