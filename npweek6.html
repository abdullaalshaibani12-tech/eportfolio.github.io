Unit 6: Statistical Inference, Confidence Intervals, and Hypothesis Testing

Unit 6 focused on the core principles of statistical inference and how sample data can be used to draw meaningful conclusions about a broader population. Building on earlier concepts, the unit reinforced the importance of working with uncertaintyâ€”an inherent part of real-world decision-making. I revisited the normal distribution and explored how it underpins many inferential techniques, providing a mathematical foundation for estimating population parameters.

A significant portion of the unit covered confidence intervals and hypothesis testing. I learned how confidence intervals offer a range of plausible values for unknown parameters, helping quantify uncertainty in estimates. The unit also explained the roles of the null and alternative hypotheses, which form the basis for structured statistical testing. Additionally, I gained a clearer understanding of significance levels and p-values, both essential for interpreting test results and guiding decision-making.

Key Learnings:

  • Revisiting inferential statistics and recognising the role of the normal distribution.
  • Understanding how confidence intervals are used to interpret and estimate population values.
  • Formulating and analysing null and alternative hypotheses.
  • Interpreting significance levels and p-values in the context of decision-making.
  • Applying hypothesis testing to support evidence-based conclusions.
