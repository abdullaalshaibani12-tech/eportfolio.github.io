Weekly Activities


Unit 4: Hybrid Agent Architecture

Unit 4 focused on hybrid agent architectures, which combine reactive and deliberative behaviors to create more flexible and efficient systems. I learned to investigate how hybrid architectures work, their underlying principles, and how they differ from purely reactive or purely deliberative systems. The unit emphasized evaluating the benefits of this approach and considering alternative architectures. A case study helped illustrate the practical deployment of a hybrid architecture and the rationale behind its selection.

Key Learnings:

  • Understanding the principles of hybrid agent architectures.
  • Assessment of benefits and limitations compared to alternative approaches.
  • Critical evaluation of architectures for solving specific problems.
  • Justification of architecture selection in practical scenarios.
