Unit 2: Exploratory Data Analysis

Unit 2 focused on understanding the principles and practices of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and its importance in data science and machine learning projects. I learned how EDA helps validate raw data, detect anomalies, and ensure that datasets are accurate and reliable for future analysis. The unit emphasized how EDA can uncover hidden insights that may not be immediately obvious to business stakeholders or data scientists, providing valuable context for decision-making. Additionally, I explored the role of EDA in defining and refining feature variables for machine learning, understanding that the process may require iterative feature engineering to optimize predictive performance. Finally, I practiced analyzing datasets using visualizations and summary statistics to gain a deep understanding of the data before applying machine learning algorithms.

Key Learnings:

  • Understanding the steps involved in performing exploratory data analysis.
  • Exploring datasets to identify key features and relationships.
  • Detecting anomalies and inconsistencies in raw data.
  • Using visual analysis to better understand data patterns and distributions.
  • Preparing and refining datasets for supervised and unsupervised machine learning.
  • Recognizing the iterative nature of feature engineering informed by EDA.
